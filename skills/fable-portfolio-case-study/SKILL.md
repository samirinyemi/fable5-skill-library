# Portfolio Case Study

## Overview

A case study sells judgment, not pixels: hiring managers and clients skim for **how you think** and whether outcomes followed. Fable's method: structure it as decisions-with-reasons, show the work at editorial scale, and cut everything that is merely "what happened next."

**REQUIRED BACKGROUND:** fable-design-dna. The page itself follows fable-editorial-design — a case study IS an editorial artifact.

## The Structure (in reading order)

1. **The hook** — one full-bleed hero of the finished work + one sentence: what it is, for whom, and the headline outcome. A skimmer stopping here should still remember you.
2. **Context card** — role, team, timeline, scope. Four lines, scannable, honest ("Sole designer" or "One of three" — inflation reads instantly).
3. **The problem as tension** — what was broken/missing and why it mattered, in the client's stakes ("support tickets doubled"), not design vocabulary ("the UX was suboptimal").
4. **Constraints** — budget, legacy, brand, deadline. Constraints are what make your decisions impressive; hiding them hides your skill.
5. **2–4 key decisions** — the heart. Each: the fork you faced, what you chose, WHY, and what it cost ("we traded onboarding depth for time-to-value"). Show the rejected direction when you can — one honest dead-end buys more trust than ten polished screens.
6. **The craft pass** — close-up crops of details that prove obsession: a type system, an empty state, motion. Editorial scale: big when it's emotional, small when it's evidence.
7. **Outcomes** — numbers if you have them, with honest attribution ("signups rose 32% in the quarter after launch — the redesign was one of several changes"). No numbers? Use qualitative proof: a user quote, adoption, what the client shipped next.
8. **Reflection** — one thing you'd do differently. Signals growth; skipping it signals defensiveness.

## Writing Rules

- Decisions, not activities: "I chose a single-column checkout because baskets were small" — never "Then I made wireframes."
- Verbs in first person singular for your work, plural for the team's. Never passive ("it was decided").
- 800–1,500 words for the main read; skimmable in 90 seconds via headlines + images alone (takeaway headlines, per fable-presentation-design).
- Kill the process theater: personas, journey maps, and sticky-note walls appear ONLY if a decision hinged on them.

## Image Curation

Every image earns its place as evidence for a claim. Real content in every shot, consistent device frames, consistent backgrounds. Lead and close with the strongest two images you have — first impression and last impression are the whole memory.

## Common Mistakes

- The chronological diary ("First I researched, then I sketched…") — structure by decision, not by week.
- All finals, no thinking — reads as a Dribbble dump; the client for judgment leaves unserved.
- Claiming solo credit for team outcomes — one reference call destroys the portfolio.
- Ten case studies of everything: three deep beats ten shallow. Curate like the accent color — scarcity is the signal.
